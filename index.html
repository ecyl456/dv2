Global Education and Socioeconomic Indicators


Education plays a significant role in society in socioeconomic factors, specifically that which surrounds employment and income. This visualisation explores factors such as unemployment and gross national income, as well as education rates across the world. Using these trends, we seek to derive a pattern across different socioeconomic factors, and how the employability and income of a population may have a correlation to each factor.

Global Unemployment Rate

We can observe from above that most of the countries observed with high unemployments seem to be focused around Africa and smaller countries in south-west Europe. The continents that seem to consistently have low unemployment rates are primarily observed in the Americas, Asia and Australia. With this information in mind, we can use this information to observe if there is a trend amongst low and high unemployment rate countries and other socioeconomic factors.

Combined Percentage of Population Between 15 and 64 Years without Education

*Averaged across 1998-2022

The bar chart shows us the top 10 countries with the highest rates of youths and adults ranged 15 to 16 which do not have education. The findings appear consistent with what the choropleth map suggests, which shows that African countries 8 out 10 countries amongst the top 10 are in Africa, while 2 are in Asia. Based on these findings, we can observe that unemployment rates and a lack of education appears to have some correlation.

Gross National Income Per Capita,
Top 10 Countries(2022)

The donut chart in comparison to the previous charts shows that countries primarily among the top 10 globally with the highest gross national income, remains largely consistent during the year 2022. Among these countries, we can observe that many are among Asia and Europe, but there are no African countries among the top 10 across the years. These findings are consitent with the trends observed so far.

Primary, Secondary & Tertiary
Enrolment Ratio

Based on the stacked bar chart, we primarily focus on the even distribution of all levels of enrolment. The top 10 countries globally are selected by their rates of tertiary education enrolment.

Amongst the countries selected, we can see that the countries which typically have an even distribution of primary, secondary and tertiary education levels still remain consistent with our findings so far. These countries mostly consists of European and Asian countries.

Global Expected Years of Schooling

Lastly, we observe globally, the expected years of schooling. From the proportional symbol map above, we can see that there is a large concentration of dark colored and larger circle marks surrounding Europe, and majority of the lighter and smaller marks appear in Africa.

Based on all these findings so far, we can conclude that there is a high correlation between education amongst the population of a country, and socioeconomic factors such as national income and unemployment.
